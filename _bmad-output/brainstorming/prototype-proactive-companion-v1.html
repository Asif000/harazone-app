Core Concept
The app IS the companion. Nameless, first-person. It watches your saved places, learns your patterns, feeds your daydreams, and keeps your travel memories alive. Three time zones: backward (nostalgia), present (proximity), forward (anticipation).
3 UI Surfaces
TICKER — low interruption. Whispers, context, history drops.
COMPANION CARD — medium. Relaunch greetings, deltas, save acks.
LOCAL NOTIFICATION — high value, sparse. Drips, anniversaries, proximity.
Key Insight
Research shows planning a trip provides as much pleasure as the trip itself. This companion EXTENDS the anticipation phase. It doesn't rush you to book — it feeds the daydream.

The Thoughtful Guide
Evocative, sensory language. Paints pictures.
That cafe in Sintra — it faces west. Late afternoon is when the light hits the valley. The pasteis de nata there use a recipe from 1837.
The Mirror Communicator
Mirrors your language style. Casual gets casual. Formal gets polished.
Casual: "yo that cafe you saved? flights just dropped 40%"

Formal: "The airfare to Porto has decreased significantly this week."
The Anticipation Architect
Deliberately extends planning pleasure. Drip-feeds details over days.
Day 1: "Saved."
Day 3: "That market opens at sunrise — locals go early for the fish auction."
Day 5: "The street behind it has a wine bar that only opens Thursdays."
The Nostalgia Curator
Keeps visited places alive. Your travel history is a living story.
1 year ago you were in that cafe in Porto. They just renovated the terrace — the new tiles match the originals from 1920.
The Pattern Matchmaker
Every save teaches the companion. Personal pattern recognition.
You keep choosing waterfront spots with outdoor seating. Here's one in Buenos Aires that has the same energy.
The Delta Reporter
Monitors saved places and surfaces CHANGES. Not static info — temporal deltas.
That rooftop in Lisbon? They added a weekend jazz night. Starts at 9pm.
The Trusted Jester
Trust is foundation, fun is spice. Humor is observational, from YOUR data.
"You've saved 6 coffee shops in Porto. At this point I think we can just call it a coffee crawl."
Trust Floor (non-negotiable)
Never hype. Never oversell. Honest about safety, cost, crowds. Admits uncertainty.
You're near that rooftop bar but it's raining and they don't have cover. Tomorrow at 4pm looks clear.
