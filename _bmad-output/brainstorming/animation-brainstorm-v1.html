Animation Brainstorm — AreaDiscovery

Click "Play" on each treatment to compare. Which FEELS right for discovering a neighborhood?

A. Mission Control — Telemetry Lock-in

Cross-pollinated from: NASA mission dashboards, satellite tracking
Chips "resolve" from blur like a signal being acquired. Numbers stabilize like instrument readings. Green flash on lock. Data feels consequential.

B. Storm Formation — Pressure Wave

Cross-pollinated from: Weather radar, atmospheric pressure maps
Chips spiral into existence like storm cells forming. Pressure wave ripples outward on arrival. Organic, slightly chaotic energy. Nature forming patterns.

C. Beat Drop — Bass Hit

Cross-pollinated from: Music visualizers, DJ software, Spotify Canvas
Chips slam in like bass drops — squash/stretch on impact, bass-line pulse underneath. Rhythmic, punchy, energetic. Each chip has IMPACT.

D. Crystallization — Particle Collapse

Cross-pollinated from: Chemistry simulations, procedural generation, magic systems
Bright scattered particles collapse and crystallize into a solid chip. Like matter forming from energy. Sparks fly off on solidification. Magical, scientific.

E. Ink Bleed — Watercolor Reveal

Cross-pollinated from: Calligraphy apps, art tools, paper textures
Chips bleed into view like ink on paper — circle reveal expanding outward, slight color saturation bloom. Organic, artful, warm. Discovery as art.

F. Glitch — Digital Artifact

Cross-pollinated from: Cyberpunk UI, hacking scenes, AR interfaces
Chips glitch into existence with RGB splits, scan lines, and stepped animation. Data being decoded from a signal. Edgy, techy, futuristic.
